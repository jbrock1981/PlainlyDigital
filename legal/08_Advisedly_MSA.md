## 1. Services

1.1 Consultant will perform cybersecurity consulting and advisory services as described in one or more Statements of Work ("SOW") executed by both parties and incorporated herein by reference.

1.2 Each SOW will specify the scope of services, deliverables, timeline, and compensation for that engagement. In the event of conflict between this Agreement and a SOW, the SOW controls for that engagement.

1.3 Consultant may use subcontractors to perform services, subject to confidentiality obligations equivalent to those in this Agreement. Consultant remains responsible for subcontractor performance.

---

## 2. Payment Terms

2.1 **Fixed-Price Engagements.** Client shall pay the fees set forth in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise stated in the SOW, fifty percent (50%) of the fixed fee is due upon execution of the SOW, and the remaining fifty percent (50%) is due upon delivery of final deliverables.

2.2 **Hourly / Retainer Engagements.** Consultant will invoice Client monthly. Payment is due within thirty (30) days of invoice date.

2.3 **Late Payments.** Amounts not paid within thirty (30) days accrue interest at 1.5% per month (or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less).

2.4 **Expenses.** Client shall reimburse Consultant for pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses (travel, software licenses, filing fees) within thirty (30) days of invoice. All reimbursable expenses require prior written approval.

2.5 **Taxes.** Fees are exclusive of applicable taxes. Client is responsible for all sales, use, or similar taxes, excluding taxes on Consultant's income.

---

## 3. Intellectual Property

3.1 **Client Deliverables.** Upon receipt of full payment, Consultant assigns to Client all right, title, and interest in work product specifically created for Client under a SOW ("Deliverables"), including any copyrights therein.

3.2 **Background IP.** Consultant retains all right, title, and interest in tools, methodologies, templates, automation scripts, AI workflows, and pre-existing intellectual property used to create Deliverables ("Background IP"). No Background IP is transferred to Client under this Agreement.

3.3 **License.** To the extent Deliverables incorporate Background IP, Consultant grants Client a non-exclusive, royalty-free, perpetual license to use such Background IP solely as incorporated in the Deliverables and solely for Client's internal compliance purposes.

3.4 **Open Source.** Deliverables will not incorporate open-source software in a manner that would require Client to disclose or license Client's proprietary information.

---

## 4. Confidentiality

4.1 Each party ("Receiving Party") shall hold the other party's ("Disclosing Party") Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not disclose it to any third party without prior written consent.

4.2 "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one party to the other that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure, including but not limited to: security assessments, gap analysis findings, system architecture details, policies, procedures, and business information.

4.3 Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement; (b) was rightfully known before disclosure; (c) is independently developed without use of Confidential Information; or (d) must be disclosed by law or court order, provided Receiving Party gives prompt written notice.

4.4 Obligations under this Section survive termination for five (5) years.

4.5 **CUI Handling.** If Client provides Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI), Client is responsible for marking CUI in accordance with applicable federal requirements. Consultant will handle any marked CUI in accordance with NIST SP 800-171 safeguarding requirements.

5.2 **Client warrants** that: (a) it has the right to enter this Agreement; (b) it will provide Consultant with accurate and complete information necessary to perform the services; (c) it owns or has rights to any materials provided to Consultant.

CONSULTANT DOES NOT WARRANT THAT DELIVERABLES WILL RESULT IN CERTIFICATION, AUTHORIZATION, OR REGULATORY APPROVAL BY ANY GOVERNMENT BODY OR ASSESSOR.

## 6. Limitation of Liability

6.1 NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

6.2 CONSULTANT'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID BY CLIENT TO CONSULTANT IN THE THREE (3) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

6.3 The limitations in this Section apply regardless of the form of action, whether in contract, tort, strict liability, or otherwise, and shall apply even if any limited remedy fails of its essential purpose.

## 7. Indemnification

7.1 **Client** shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Consultant from any claims, damages, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from: (a) Client's breach of this Agreement; (b) Client's use of Deliverables in violation of this Agreement or applicable law; (c) any materials provided by Client that infringe third-party rights.

7.2 **Consultant** shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client from any claims arising from Consultant's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

---

## 8. Term and Termination

8.1 **Term.** This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated.

8.2 **Termination for Convenience.** Either party may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days written notice. Termination does not affect active SOWs unless the SOW is separately terminated.

8.3 **Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate immediately upon written notice if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within fifteen (15) days of written notice.

8.4 **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination: (a) Client shall pay all fees for services rendered through the termination date; (b) for fixed-price engagements terminated for convenience by Client, the deposit is non-refundable and Client owes a pro-rated fee for work completed; (c) each party shall return or destroy the other's Confidential Information upon request.

---

## 9. Independent Contractor

9.1 Consultant is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship. Consultant is solely responsible for its own taxes, insurance, and benefits.

9.2 Consultant retains the right to perform services for other clients, provided such work does not conflict with obligations under this Agreement.

---

## 10. General Provisions

10.1 **Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Tennessee, without regard to conflict of law principles.

10.2 **Dispute Resolution.** The parties will attempt to resolve disputes through good-faith negotiation. If unresolved within thirty (30) days, disputes shall be submitted to binding arbitration in Giles County, Tennessee under the rules of the American Arbitration Association.

10.3 **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with all executed SOWs, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements.

10.4 **Amendments.** Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.

10.5 **Severability.** If any provision is found unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force.

10.6 **No Waiver.** Failure to enforce any provision does not constitute a waiver of future enforcement.

10.7 **Notices.** Notices must be in writing and delivered by email with confirmation or overnight courier to the addresses above.

10.8 **Force Majeure.** Neither party is liable for delays caused by circumstances beyond its reasonable control.
